Library Journal Movers & Shakers: A 25-Year Historical Record is an open, recoverable dataset of the library and information science profession's own self-recognition, 2002–2026. It is one of three projects in the LIS Historical Commons, currently underway as adaptive infophilia work.

Now in their 25th year, the LJ Movers & Shakers awards are one of the profession's most sustained acts of self-recognition in the 21st century. Taken together across 25 cohorts, they constitute a historical record: of who the field honored, what kinds of work it called exemplary, how its categories of recognition shifted — from Freedom Fighters and Crusaders to Ban Battlers and Team Players — and where, geographically, that work was happening.

While previous efforts have documented or visualized portions of the record, no single open, searchable, and mappable resource appears to have brought the full history together in one place. The data is incomplete and improvable. Contributions and corrections are welcome.

LJ has evolved its category names over 25 years, reflecting how the field's priorities have shifted. Freedom Fighters was active 2007–2009, rolled into the broader "Activists" umbrella.

Note on revoked awards. A number of honorees across multiple years — visible on LJ's own Movers by Year page as "(award revoked at request of recipient)" — requested removal of their recognition. The revocations followed professional controversy surrounding a Library Journal institutional award to Seattle Public Library, which prompted several individual Movers honorees to disassociate from the program in solidarity with concerns raised at the time. This dataset follows LJ's treatment: revoked names are excluded. Researchers reconstructing the full historical record should consult the LJ archive directly and note the years affected: 2004, 2006, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011.
