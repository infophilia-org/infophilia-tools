dLIST

Digital Library of Information Science and Technology

2002 — 2013 · University of Arizona

Working Bibliography Community Record Compiled by Anita Sundaram Coleman · May 2026
About Funding Community Publications Awards Legacy
About dLIST. dLIST was an early open-access LIS repository built on EPrints software that helped demonstrate OAI-PMH metadata harvesting, interoperability, and federated searching for scholarly discovery — connecting research, education and practice communities across the disparate Information Studies disciplines, including Archival Science, Library and Information Science, Museum Informatics, and Information Systems. Beyond building infrastructure, dLIST trained LIS students for digital repository work — in metadata, copyright, and scholarly communication — at a time when such training was rare and the profession had few models for it. Using PKP Harvester software with improvements developed by GRA Joseph Roback, the dLIST team built DL-Harvest — an open access aggregator for LIS drawing on dLIST and 13 other OAI-PMH compliant archives. dLIST also pioneered RSS feeds for repository updates and developed a Latest News feature — both novel for scholarly repositories in 2004–2005, before social media and before the iPhone. The repository was preserved in the University of Arizona Campus Repository when it closed to new submissions in 2013. This is its working bibliography and community record.
Timeline

2001

An independent study supervised by Anita Sundaram Coleman at the University of Arizona opens the inquiry that becomes dLIST. The project begins with a student's question.

2002

dLIST launches on EPrints2 software, OAI-PMH v2.0 compliant. Seed funding from UA College of Social and Behavioral Sciences and Proposition 301 / Arizona Center for Information Science and Technology. A $5,000 server grant. Core faculty team: Coleman (SIRLS), Malone (SIRLS/JELIS co-editor), Bracke (AHSL). First ECE GRA (Gaurav Gupta) begins technical development. This is pre-social media, pre-iPhone: RSS feeds and Latest News features are genuinely novel for scholarly repositories.

2003

CALIBER 2003 presentation, Ahmedabad, India. NSF NSDL grant application (~$400,000) rejected. Karthik Subramaniam (ECE) joins as second GRA. OAI-PMH integration work deepens. Educause NLII presentation, New Orleans.

2004–2006

TRIF grant awarded: $21,397 from State of Arizona for "Information Technology & Society Research Lab: The Study of Scholarly Communication in Digital Repositories." Coleman PI. All money goes to GRAs and servers. Joseph Roback (CS) joins — develops OAI-PMH implementation and EPrints patches that become dLIST's technical foundation; builds DL-Harvest using PKP Harvester with flow control, sets, and advanced searching improvements. JingFeng Xia joins as metadata and copyright editor. Shawn Nelson contributes to metadata and collections. UA library declines to host server; Paul Bracke moves it to Arizona Health Sciences Library. JCDL 2004, Tucson. ITS Research Lab presentation. IFLA 2005 poster, Oslo. ALISE 2005 poster, Boston. ASIS&T 2005, Charlotte (multiple presentations). By late 2005: fewer than 500 documents, just over 700 registered users — growth slow, solutions tried ranging from outreach to R&D.

2005

IMLS National Leadership Grant application rejected. DL-Harvest aggregator launched publicly. Advisory Board established — members include Michael Gorman (Dean, California State Fresno). dLIST 2005 scholarly communication survey launched. Announced in D-Lib Magazine In Brief, October 2005.

2006–2007

dLIST Classics project launches with Proposition 301 / ACIST funding — specifically allocated for digitization. Phase 1 (May 2006): Five Laws of Library Science digitized by Michael May, Carnegie-Stout Public Library, Dubuque, Iowa — a public librarian from Iowa making India's foundational library science text openly accessible globally. Permission granted by SRELS (Neelameghan, Prasad, Raghavan, DRTC) and Advisory Board member Arunachalam. Phase 2 (Fall 2006): five additional Ranganathan titles, digitized by Joy Wilcox, SIRLS. Phase 3 (Spring 2007): coordinated by Cheryl K. Malone — four more Ranganathan titles plus three additional classics including Pauline Atherton's Putting Knowledge to Work. Digitizer: Susan Ditch; Quality Control: Megan Plesea; Digitization Training: Han Yan; Book Editor: Barbara Hutchinson. Twelve titles total. ECDL Alicante (team presentation). 9th ISKO Vienna. OR2007, San Antonio. Dominika Sokol presents marketing paper at BOBCATSSS, Prague. By 2007: 800 documents, 1,200 registered users. Coleman named Library Journal Movers and Shakers 2007 — "Global Thinker." Nominated by Kristin Eschenfelder, University of Wisconsin.

2013

dLIST closes to new submissions. Preserved in UA Campus Repository. E-LIS (the Italian subject repository that ran parallel to dLIST, with whom Coleman had attempted collaboration without success) later becomes effectively non-functional. The disciplinary repository model dLIST pioneered has not been sustained.

2026

dLIST story told in ALA150 blog post: "The Future of Librarians: dLIST, A Library Story for the Age of AI." Gaurav Gupta (AWS) appears to have presented on Edge AI and Physical AI themes at AWS re:Invent 2025. Joseph Roback (NVIDIA) works in the same AI infrastructure ecosystem. Both started by learning copyright and building OAI-PMH infrastructure at dLIST.

Funding Record The complete picture

dLIST ran on $21,397 in state technology funding plus seed money and graduate student labor. The federal grants that would have sustained it were declined. The infrastructure it proved is now the backbone of library discovery worldwide.

The dLIST Community Those who built it

Anita Sundaram Coleman

Founder & Principal Investigator

SIRLS, University of Arizona. Conceived dLIST from an independent study she supervised. Led the project 2002–2013. JELIS co-editor. Copyright Transfer Agreement research. Library Journal Movers & Shakers 2007 — "Global Thinker." TRIF grant PI.

Now: Independent Scholar, Infophilia. Adjunct Lecturer, iSchool Illinois.

Paul Bracke

Server Administrator & AHSL Representative

Arizona Health Sciences Library. Moved the dLIST server to AHSL when UA library would not support it — an act that saved the project. Core team member throughout.

Now: Academic librarian. Subsequently moved to Wayne State University.

Cheryl Knott Malone

Faculty Editor & JELIS Co-Editor

SIRLS, University of Arizona. Faculty editor on dLIST. Co-editor of JELIS (Journal of Education for Library and Information Science) alongside Coleman. Co-author of IMLS grant proposal. ALISE 2005 poster co-presenter.

Gaurav Gupta

ECE GRA — First Technical Iteration

Electrical and Computer Engineering, UA, 2002–2003. Built dLIST's first technical infrastructure. Learned copyright and self-archiving practices as part of the dLIST team. 18 granted patents.

Now: WW Principal Partner Solutions Architect, IoT & Physical AI, AWS. 18 granted patents. Appears to have presented on Edge AI and Physical AI themes at AWS re:Invent 2025, Las Vegas, based on LinkedIn post.

Now: Staff Software Engineer, Samsara (IoT). Previously Senior Software Engineer, Amazon (11 years); Garmin ADAS.

Adjacent Work — Pre-dLIST & NSDL Network

dLIST emerged from Coleman's broader digital library research at the University of Arizona in the late 1990s and early 2000s, which included work on the Alexandria Digital Library (geospatial), DLESE (earth science education), GROW-NCERL (civil engineering education), and the NSDL network. These projects established the research context and interdisciplinary collaborations from which dLIST grew.
